Global Overview of Vaccination Trends

This section visualizes global vaccination efforts using a choropleth map and a bar chart. The map shows vaccination rates by country, while the chart compares regional vaccination coverage.

Choropleth Map

Explanation: The map uses shades of blue to represent vaccination rates. Lighter blue indicates lower vaccination rates, while darker blue indicates higher vaccination rates. Hover over a country to see the exact percentage of the population vaccinated.

Bar Chart

Explanation: This chart compares average vaccination coverage across regions. The height of each bar indicates the percentage of vaccinated people in the region. All bars are styled in blue for consistency.

Vaccination Progress Over Time

Track the progress of global vaccination efforts and how they have evolved over time.

Line Chart

Explanation: The line chart illustrates the cumulative number of vaccinations administered globally over time. It helps identify trends and key milestones.

Stacked Area Chart

Explanation: The stacked area chart shows the proportion of fully vaccinated, partially vaccinated, and booster doses over time. It highlights the shift in focus as campaigns progress.

Vaccination Trends by Age Groups

This heatmap visualizes the vaccination rates across age groups over time. Each cell represents a specific vaccination rate for an age group on a given date:

  • Light Blue: Low vaccination rates (0% to 25%)
  • Medium Blue: Moderate vaccination rates (26% to 75%)
  • Dark Blue: High vaccination rates (76% to 100%)
Use this chart to identify trends, such as which age groups reached high vaccination rates quickly and which lagged behind.

Socioeconomic Factors and Vaccination

Explore how socioeconomic factors such as healthcare expenditure, income levels, and regions affect vaccination rates. These visualizations highlight disparities and correlations.

Heatmap

This heatmap visualizes the relationship between vaccination rates (per million) and socioeconomic factors (Gini Index) for selected countries. Countries with higher Gini Index values generally indicate greater income inequality. Hover over a rectangle to see detailed data for a specific country.

Grouped Bar Chart

This grouped bar chart allows you to compare vaccination rates (per million) and Gini Index values (income inequality) for selected countries. Each country is represented as a group of two bars: one for vaccination rates and one for Gini Index. Hover over the bars to see detailed information for each country.

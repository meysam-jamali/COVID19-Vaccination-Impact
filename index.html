Global Overview of Vaccination Trends

This section visualizes global vaccination efforts using a choropleth map and a bar chart. The map shows vaccination rates by country, while the chart compares regional vaccination coverage.

Choropleth Map

Explanation: The map uses shades of blue to represent vaccination rates. Lighter blue indicates lower vaccination rates, while darker blue indicates higher vaccination rates. Hover over a country to see the exact percentage of the population vaccinated.

Bar Chart

Explanation: This chart compares average vaccination coverage across regions. The height of each bar indicates the percentage of vaccinated people in the region. All bars are styled in blue for consistency.

Vaccination Progress Over Time

Track the progress of global vaccination efforts and how they have evolved over time.

Line Chart

Explanation: The line chart illustrates the cumulative number of vaccinations administered globally over time. It helps identify trends and key milestones.

Stacked Area Chart

Explanation: The stacked area chart shows the proportion of fully vaccinated, partially vaccinated, and booster doses over time. It highlights the shift in focus as campaigns progress.

Vaccination, COVID-19 Deaths, and Hospitalizations

This section explores the relationship between vaccination rates and their impact on COVID-19 deaths and hospitalizations. The charts below provide insights into trends across key countries over the years.

Vaccination vs. COVID-19 Deaths

This chart shows the annual COVID-19 deaths (2020–2023) alongside vaccination rates. It highlights the trends in deaths reported each year and allows comparisons across countries. While higher vaccination rates may correlate with fewer deaths, the data also reflects other factors such as healthcare infrastructure and pandemic response strategies.

Vaccination vs. Hospitalization Rates

This chart highlights the relationship between vaccination rates and hospitalization trends due to COVID-19. Higher vaccination rates are often associated with reduced hospital admissions, easing the burden on healthcare systems.

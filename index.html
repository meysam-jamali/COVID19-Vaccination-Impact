Socioeconomic Factors and Vaccination

Explore how socioeconomic factors such as healthcare expenditure, income levels, and regions affect vaccination rates. These visualizations highlight disparities and correlations.

Heatmap

This heatmap visualizes the relationship between vaccination rates (per million) and socioeconomic factors (Gini Index) for selected countries. Countries with higher Gini Index values generally indicate greater income inequality. Hover over a rectangle to see detailed data for a specific country.

Grouped Bar Chart

This grouped bar chart allows you to compare vaccination rates (per million) and Gini Index values (income inequality) for selected countries. Each country is represented as a group of two bars: one for vaccination rates and one for Gini Index. Hover over the bars to see detailed information for each country.

Gender and Health Factors

This diverging bar chart highlights the relationship between smoking prevalence (male and female) and vaccination rates for selected countries. The focus is on key nations to provide clear and actionable insights into gender-based health factors affecting vaccination trends.

Diverging Bar Chart: Vaccination vs. Smoking Prevalence

Explanation: Bars extending to the left represent vaccination rates for female smokers, while bars extending to the right represent vaccination rates for male smokers. This focused view makes it easier to identify key trends among selected countries.

Life Expectancy and Vaccination Rates

This bar chart represents the average vaccination rates (per million) categorized by life expectancy ranges. It highlights how vaccination rates vary among nations with differing levels of life expectancy.

▮ Average Vaccination Rate (per million)

“The x-axis displays life expectancy ranges (e.g., 60–64 years), representing countries where the average life expectancy falls within this range—not the age of individuals. The y-axis shows the average vaccination rate per million people for countries within each range. The chart is sorted in ascending order by life expectancy, making it easy to compare vaccination rates across different ranges.”

Role of Government Policies

This visualization explores the relationship between the stringency of COVID-19 policies (e.g., lockdowns, mask mandates) and vaccination rates. By comparing vaccination rates against the stringency index, we can assess whether stricter policies encouraged or hindered vaccination efforts.

The x-axis represents the stringency index (0–100), which measures the strictness of government policies, while the y-axis displays the vaccination rates (per million people). This chart helps evaluate how policy strictness correlates with vaccination rates across countries.

Vaccination and Economic Recovery

This box plot visualizes the distribution of GDP per capita across vaccination rate intervals. The red line represents the average GDP per capita for each group.

By observing this chart, we can understand how vaccination rates correlate with economic recovery.

Key Insights: Higher vaccination rates may correlate with improved economic performance. Use this chart to identify outliers or patterns within specific vaccination ranges and time periods.
